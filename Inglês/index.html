About France

The culture of France has been shaped by geographical features, historical events and the influence of internal groups. The country, and especially Paris, the capital, has played an important role as a cultural mirror of Europe (since the 17th century) and the world (since the 19th century). Since the early 19th century, France has also been an exporter of cinema, fashion, cuisine and art, and has greatly influenced the West also in the fields of politics and economics. French culture today is marked by major socio-economic and regional differences and strong unionist tendencies.

It is estimated that the French spend an average of 1075 euros per year on their cultural activities. According to the same source, the cultural activities most sought after by the French are: cinema (50%), visits to museums and monuments (35%), visits to exhibitions (25%), amateur shows (20%), theatre (16%), circus (13%), theme parks (11%), rock, jazz or classical music shows (9%) and opera (3%).
